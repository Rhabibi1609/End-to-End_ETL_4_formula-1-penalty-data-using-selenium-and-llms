11 - 13 April 2025
From The FIA Formula One Technical Delegate Document 56
To The Stewards Date 13 April 2025
Time 22:10
Technical Delegate’s Report
Before the Race:
A spare RHS front corner of car numbers 31 and 87 was digitally scanned and the scanned data
were compared with the team declared CAD drawings. Further the team declared CAD drawings
were also compared with previously submitted versions and checked for conformance with the 2025
Formula One Technical Regulations.
The front wing of car number 06 was digitally scanned and the scanned data were compared with the
team declared CAD drawings. Further the team declared CAD drawings were also compared with
previously submitted versions and checked for conformance with the 2025 Formula One Technical
Regulations.
The front wing flap adjustable range of car number 06 was digitally checked.
The thickness, the density and the hardness of the skids of car numbers 18, 14, 10, 07, 87, 06 and
30 were checked.
A vertical and angled rear wing main plane deflection test was carried out on car numbers 81 and 55.
A rear wing main plane tip deflection test was carried out on car numbers 81 and 55.
A front floor deflection test was carried on car number 16.
The height of the front wing gurneys were checked on car numbers 81, 04, 16, 44, 01, 22, 31, 87
and 05.
The height of the rear wing gurney were checked on car numbers 81, 04, 16, 44, 01, 22, 63, 12, 18,
14, 10, 07, 31, 87, 06, 30, 27 and 05.
The ICE air intake system and the cooling system of car number 10 were checked.
Clutch paddle linearity checks have been carried out on cars 44 and 22.
On the grid it was checked that all cars had fitted their tyres and the tyre heating blankets were
disconnected, when the “5-Minutes” signal was given.
The tyre control box settings were checked on the grid for the race start tyre sets of all cars.
On the grid the minimum tyre starting pressure of the LHS and RHS front and rear tyre was checked
on all cars.

The steering wheel of all cars has been checked.

Single clutch paddle use for the race start has been checked on all cars.
It was checked that no car exceeded 80 km/h when leaving the formation grid prior to the start of the
race.
It was verified on all cars that the PCU dash display configuration was not changed during Parc
Fermé.
The tyre starting pressures of all cars during the race were checked.
The tyres used by all drivers during the race today have been checked.

checked were found to be in conformity with the 2025 FIA Formula One Technical Regulations.
